A synthesis of 17 anonymous field reports from four Syrian cities shows accelerating food insecurity. Respondents in Aleppo and Idlib describe deteriorating access to basic goods, with 41% reporting displacement in the past 30 days. The pattern mirrors early-stage supply chain collapse seen in Darfur earlier this year.

## Key findings

Bread prices in Aleppo have surged 14% over the past 30 days, driven by narrowing supply corridors and increased transport costs. Field respondents describe a market where basic staples are available but increasingly unaffordable for displaced populations.

The Early Warning Score for Syria now stands at 39/100, reflecting deteriorating conditions across multiple indicators. While this remains below the critical 51-point threshold, the trajectory is concerning — the score has risen from 28 three months ago.

## Data context

This brief draws on 17 anonymous field responses collected between 10–17 March 2026 from Aleppo, Idlib, Damascus, and Homs. Respondents were self-selected through the CSIORS KoboToolbox survey. Sample size limitations apply — findings should be interpreted as indicative signals, not statistically representative measures.
